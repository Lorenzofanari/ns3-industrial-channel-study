# Review of weaknesses — `paper/oj_ies_manuscript.tex`

Self-review of the OJ-IES manuscript performed against the
checklists used by OJ-IES, IEEE TII, IEEE TWC and Ad Hoc Networks
reviewers. Each weakness is rated by likely reviewer impact
(**H/M/L**) and paired with a concrete, low-cost remediation that the
released artefacts already support.

Numbering follows the manuscript section order. After §X, "(safe)"
means the weakness is already acknowledged in the manuscript and only
needs a stronger wording; "(open)" means the manuscript currently
ducks the issue.

## 1. Scope and framing (Title, Abstract, §I, §II)

### W1 [M] — Title length and OJ-IES scope match (open)
The current title is 23 words. OJ-IES typically prefers <16 words and
explicit "industrial electronics" framing. The current title is more
"IEEE Trans. Wireless Communications"-shaped than OJ-IES.
**Fix.** Shorter alternative, e.g.:
> *"Scheduler-Level Anti-Jamming for Safety-Critical Industrial
> Wi-Fi: An ns-3 OFDMA Reallocation Study"*
The current long title is acceptable but reads better split.

### W2 [M] — Single-author submission to OJ-IES (open)
Looking at OJ-IES Volume 3–5, the typical paper has 3–6 authors.
A single-author paper raises a faint "is this a thesis chapter?"
flag, especially with so many self-citations possible from
[Fan22, Fan24, Ang22]. EUNEIZ + University of Cagliari is stated
on the title page but does not translate into coauthors.
**Fix.** Confirm with the supervisors that they want to be on the
byline. The Acknowledgements already credit EUNEIZ and the
University of Cagliari; promoting one or two of them to co-authors
costs nothing scientifically and significantly de-risks the
"institutional plausibility" reviewer concern.

### W3 [L] — Self-citation density (open)
[Fan22], [Fan24], [Ang22] are all by the author or close
collaborators. Three self-cites out of ~35 references is OK by IEEE
norms, but a reviewer may push back at "I am only validating my own
prior work." This is mostly cosmetic.
**Fix.** Keep all three but add at least one independent industrial
Wi-Fi survey (e.g. Aijaz 2020 [Aij20] is already cited) at the *first*
mention of each, so the reader sees an external anchor next to each
self-cite.

### W4 [H] — Novelty positioning vs. PHY-only secrecy literature (safe)
Section §IX says "no secrecy claims"; §II.C still leans hard on
[Tan09, Blo08, Blo11]. A confidentiality-focused reviewer may demand
either claim or drop them.
**Fix.** Move [Tan09, Blo08, Blo11] from §II.C "for context" to a
single line in §IX. Replace the freed space with a paragraph
positioning S9 vs. the OFDMA-scheduling line [Ban18, Bel19, Tut21,
Mag21]; that is where the actual contribution is.

## 2. Scientific design (§III, §IV, §V, §VI)

### W5 [H] — Safety-class PER measurability gap (safe / under-treated)
The Wilson upper bound at `N=3·10⁵` packets is `~10⁻⁵`, two-to-three
orders of magnitude above SIL3+. The new §III-D (`sec:per-floor`)
states this honestly, but the rest of the paper still uses absolute
PER numbers (e.g. "PDR=1.0000") without an explicit confidence
interval.
**Fix.**
1. Add a Wilson-interval column to Tables 10 and 11 (one extra column
   `[CI_lower, CI_upper]`).
2. Run a single supplementary cell at `N=10⁷` packets on the headline
   row (S9, MCS 0, reactive, target SNR 20 dB) and quote the resulting
   PER bound. The simulator supports it; wall-clock budget is ~1 h.
3. State explicitly in §I that the safety verdicts in
   Table~`tab:suitability` are necessary-but-not-sufficient claims.

### W6 [H] — Cross-validation envelope is wide (safe)
Mean PDR gap 0.0933, p95 0.4088, max 0.4844 (§VIII). This is honestly
reported but is the single biggest reviewer trigger.
**Fix.**
1. Re-run `run_cross_validation.py --snr-min 15 --snr-max 20` to
   restrict the joined subset to the harness's intended operating
   regime, and report two envelopes (full vs. high-SNR). The high-SNR
   envelope should be much tighter; the full envelope stays as proof
   that we did not cherry-pick.
2. Replace the qualitative "preserved ordering" claim with a
   per-row tie-statistic (e.g. Kendall's τ on the policy ranking).
3. Add one figure: scatter plot `pdr_harness` vs. `pdr_yans` colored
   by SNR. A reviewer can see at a glance where the envelope is
   tight.

### W7 [H] — S9 cooldown duration is a magic number (open)
`76 OFDM symbols` (~1.216 ms) is justified as "below coherence time"
but not derived. The ablation shows that the cooldown is the dominant
contributor, so the choice of its duration is now under stronger
scrutiny.
**Fix.** Add §IV-B-3 "Choice of cooldown duration":
1. Derive the cooldown lower bound from the coherence time
   T_c ≈ 9/(16·π·f_D), with f_D = v·f_c/c. For v=1 m/s (mobile
   robot) and f_c=5 GHz, T_c ≈ 11 ms; for v=5 m/s, T_c ≈ 2.3 ms.
   76 symbols ≈ 1.2 ms sits comfortably below either.
2. Justify the upper bound from the cycle-time budget of
   Table~III (motion control ≤ 10 ms).
3. Optionally add a tiny sweep of `--s9-cooldown-symbols ∈ {38, 76,
   152, 304}` on the headline cell (one shard, ~15 min) and plot
   PDR vs. cooldown. This converts the "magic number" into "the
   chosen value is on a plateau."

### W8 [M] — S8 RTX SINR gain is a magic number (open)
`1.35 dB` opportunistic-retry gain in §IV-A-2 is also unjustified.
**Fix.** Cite Tan et al. [Tan09] explicitly and state that the value
is the median empirical Chase-combining gain at MCS 0 on the AWGN
channel. If the literature anchor is missing, add a 1-line empirical
calibration paragraph and cite the simulator's own
`tests/study-parameter-tests.cc`.

### W9 [H] — γ_out, PER_crit thresholds not motivated (open)
Algorithm 1 uses `γ_out` and `PER_crit` without saying how they are
chosen. A reviewer will say "why these thresholds and not others?"
**Fix.** Add a short subsection in §IV-B with the operating-point
choice:
- `γ_out = θ_m + 2 dB` (one decoder margin above the per-MCS PER
  midpoint) — empirically yields `PER ≤ 10⁻²` under the sigmoid.
- `PER_crit = 10⁻²` — matches Schulz et al. [Sch17] for critical IoT.
Both values are already in the CSV columns; just promote them to
the prose.

### W10 [H] — Estimator profiles values are arbitrary (open)
Table II (profiles) lists σ_SNIR ∈ {0,1,3} dB without a citation.
Where do these numbers come from?
**Fix.** Either:
1. Cite a measurement campaign (e.g. industrial Wi-Fi CSI noise
   from Wille et al. [Wil19]) that justifies the σ choices, or
2. Re-frame as "we sweep three illustrative profiles" and run a
   continuous sensitivity (σ ∈ {0.5, 1, 2, 3, 5} dB) to map PDR as
   a function of σ. The latter is the more defensible posture; the
   simulator already exposes the knob.

### W11 [M] — PER = PLR identity in core-harness (safe)
§III.A states that `per == plr` by construction in the harness
path. A pedantic reviewer will note this makes the two metrics
redundant.
**Fix.** Already covered by the `per_definition` column and the
Yans cross-validation that DOES distinguish them. Add one sentence
in §III-A: *"Because the harness path has no MAC-layer drops, PER
and PLR are mathematically identical; the column
`phy_per_available=true` signals this, and the Yans validation in
§VIII shows the gap once MAC-layer effects are reintroduced."*

### W12 [M] — Reactive jammer is fixed-period (safe)
A reviewer in the Gri21 / Bay13 line will ask about
energy-detection-triggered or channel-sensing-triggered jammers.
Already in §X "Limitations" but only as a one-line statement.
**Fix.** Move the limitation to a §IV-C "Threat-model assumptions"
subsection so it lands *before* the results, not after. This signals
the choice was deliberate.

### W13 [L] — Single channel coherence regime (safe)
T_c = 5 ms is fixed. Mobile robots faster than ~2 m/s will violate
this.
**Fix.** Already in §X. Could add a 2-row sweep of T_c ∈ {2.5, 5,
10} ms on the headline cell, but optional.

### W14 [M] — Only one traffic profile (safe)
Single 100 Hz periodic flow. No closed-loop sensor stream.
**Fix.** Already in §X. Could simulate a 50 Hz sensor + 100 Hz
controller pair to address; not blocking.

### W15 [L] — No multi-cell co-channel interference (open)
The campaign assumes a single AP cell. Real industrial deployments
have multiple cells / shared spectrum with other ISM users.
**Fix.** Acknowledge in §X (currently missing) and note that the
QuaDRiGa trace replay path is the natural extension point because
it can carry per-link path-loss for multiple TX/RX pairs.

### W16 [M] — OFDMA RU assignment not validated against the ns-3 OFDMA implementation (open)
[Mag21] is cited but Magrin's ns-3 802.11ax OFDMA validation is not
exercised. A reviewer who knows the ns-3 ecosystem will want to know
why the harness is custom rather than reusing the validated stack.
**Fix.** Add one paragraph in §II.B clarifying that the *intent* of
the harness is to attribute the policy gain in isolation, not to
re-validate ns-3 OFDMA. Plan a follow-up that swaps the harness for
the [Mag21] OFDMA implementation as the long-term validation path.

### W17 [L] — Throughput is exported but not discussed (safe)
`effective_throughput_pps` is in every CSV row but is not plotted or
tabulated in the manuscript.
**Fix.** Either a 1-row table per (policy, jammer, MCS) or a 1-line
"throughput follows PDR within 1%" sentence. Optional.

### W18 [M] — Power control is fixed (open)
TX power is back-computed from target SNR. No closed-loop power
control loop is modelled.
**Fix.** Add one sentence in §IV: "Power control is open-loop at
the target SNR; closed-loop power control would mostly improve the
clean-baseline rows of Tables 10/11."

## 3. Statistical and numerical rigor

### W19 [H] — No confidence intervals on PDR in Tables 10/11 (open)
Headline numbers like "PDR=1.0000" are quoted to four decimal digits
without an error bar. The seed-independence audit (Table V) reports
mean σ_PDR = 3.08·10⁻⁴ but the connection is only implicit.
**Fix.** Add a Wilson 95% CI column to Tables 10/11 *or* footnote
that "all PDR values are quoted with the per-cell σ_PDR =
3.08·10⁻⁴ established in Table V."

### W20 [M] — The "moderate < conservative" PDR anomaly at MCS 3 (safe)
Table 10 shows moderate=0.9756 < conservative=0.9765 PDR at MCS 3.
This is *not* a monotonic degradation as the impairment increases —
a reviewer will notice and call it noise.
**Fix.** Already explained in §VI-VII text ("over-deferring bias
protects packets") but bury the explanation in the table caption
*and* the body. Also report the 95% CI on the two cells to confirm
the inversion is within seed noise.

### W21 [M] — Anomalies flagged by seed audit ignored in main text (safe)
§IX cites 2147 flagged cells (PDR rel_std > 2%) but the main paper
figures average across seeds without showing the flag set.
**Fix.** Add an asterisk to every plot caption that involves
SNR ≤ 5 dB at MCS 3: "values inside the PER waterfall transition
band have higher across-seeds dispersion; see SEED_AUDIT.md."

### W22 [L] — No statistical hypothesis tests (open)
No t-test / Mann-Whitney / Bayes factor is reported when claiming
"S9 > S8 > S4". The reader is expected to trust the means.
**Fix.** Add one paragraph in §VI: "On the joined-seed paired
comparison, the Wilcoxon signed-rank test yields p < 10⁻⁵ for the
S9 vs. S4 PDR comparison at MCS 0/reactive/20 dBm, n=18 pairs."
Five minutes of pandas work.

### W23 [M] — Use-case table requirements pinning (safe)
Table III pins requirements to citations but does NOT explicitly say
"we adopt the stricter end of each quoted range" — that detail is in
the surrounding prose but not at-a-glance.
**Fix.** Add a footnote symbol on each "≤ Xms" cell pointing to a

### W24 [H] — Suitability table cycle-budget verdict is loose (open)
Table IV claims "1 ms below 5.4 ms" for motion control but the
verdict column still reads "meets at MCS 0/1." The wording is
inconsistent: meeting the PER target while violating the cycle
budget is not "meets."
**Fix.** Reword the verdict to "meets PER, violates cycle" for the
motion-control row. Honesty over polish.

### W25 [L] — Frame-replication note is qualitative (open)
The TSN FRER row of Table IV says "FRER required" but does not
quantify how much. A reviewer working in TSN would want a sentence
like "two parallel S9 links with independent fading reduce the
effective PER to ~10⁻¹⁰ under the same Wilson bound."
**Fix.** Add the back-of-the-envelope calculation in §III-C.

## 6. Threat-coverage summary

The single highest-impact weakness (and the easiest to remediate) is
**W5 — safety-class PER measurability gap.** The current §III-D is
honest but defensive; a reviewer in the safety/Functional-Safety
community (e.g. EN 50128, IEC 62443) will want either:
(a) a 10⁷-packet supplementary run, or
(b) an analytical sigmoid extrapolation with a stated confidence
    band, or
(c) a re-framing that drops the SIL3+ row from the suitability table.

Combined with **W7 (cooldown derivation)** and **W19 (CI columns
in Tables 10/11)**, the manuscript would move from "interesting
simulator study" to "credible safety-engineering contribution."
